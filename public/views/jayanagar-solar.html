How solar works




Grid-tied solar power systems with net-metering

  • Grid-tied solar power systems with net-metering is the most common type of system for most power consumers all over India
  • In grid-tied systems, solar power is always given preference over both grid and diesel genset. An intelligent solar inverter ensures that solar is first fully utilized and then remaining power requirement is drawn from the grid or diesel generator
  • In the Indian context, due to power cuts, the diesel generator has to run throughout the day to meet critical loads, in such situation, this type of system is most viable. It saves on both electricity cost and diesel bills
  • This system is connected to the mains (LT Panel) and become a seamless part of the grid.
  • When solar generation is more than consumption, excess power is exported to the grid. This exported power is adjusted when the solar power generation is lesser than the consumption in the premises.
  • Savings from the system lead to a payback period of up to 5 years. Thereafter, you get free power for 15 more years.
  • For Patna and other places where electricity is almost regular, we recommend this type of system.
  • However, for Darbhanga, wher electricity is not regular, we recommend Hybrid type of system.
  • It is important to note that this type of system will not run if the grid is down and the diesel generator is also not running.
  • Subsidy based rooftop projects need to use 'Made in India' modules, we prefer to use - Adani Solar, Vikram Solar, Waaree Solar,Tata Solar and Sova Solar.
