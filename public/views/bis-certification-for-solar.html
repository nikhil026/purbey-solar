Confusion around BIS certification for solar inverters

12 December 2018 (Update) - Inverter manufacturers, sharing the same fate as the module suppliers, are struggling to gain some clarity on the Government policies for BIS certification.



Q) What is the need for BIS certification?

A) The Indian Solar market has expanded impressively over the past few years and as the market has grown, the Government has begun to pay attention to quality issues. The concern is valid as solar projects ar expected to last 25 years, it is therefore important to make sure that they are built using quality products including modules and inverters.

Q) Why now ?

A) Realising the issue, the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE) recently issued guidelines for conducting tests on power inverters for use in photovoltaic (PV) power systems and utility-interconnected PV Inverters.

Q) What is the notificaton ?

A) The Government has mandated that the tests must be conducted by labs for compulsory registration with the Rureau of Indian Standards (BIS) for the implementation of the order titled Solar Photovoltaic systems, Devices and Component Goods Order 2017.

Q) What is the deadline ?

A) The notification was released in September 2017, the dedline has been extended several times as the industry has been seeking more and more time for the compliance of the order. The latest deadline for the compliance of the order is January 1, 2019.

Q) What is the problem faced ?

A) As informed by a supplier not wanting to be named "There is confusion between CPRI and MNRE notification, labs are charging exhorbitant testing fee per model. It becomes highly difficult to justify the charges based on sales volume for a particular model, lack of test labs and their testing capabilities are another areaa of concern, at present, only CPRI is the only testing centre available to test inverters for BIS certification and even they are not properly equipped."

Q) What awaits the future ?

A) Though the inverter manufactureres and suppliers are willing to follow BIS certification, the same is difficult in the absence of equipment for test labs. The authorities need to understand the availability of labs, manpower and capabilities before picking a new deadline. On the other hand, a CPRI official involved with BIS certification said that they are ready with the testing and can test upto 60 kW of inverters, testing facilities upto 200 kW and 500 kW is not ready and may take another six months."
